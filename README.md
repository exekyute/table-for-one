# Table for One

A concept food blog: a quiet diary kept from the eating side of the table rather than the kitchen. Each post is a short, unhurried note about a single meal, where it was, and how it landed, set against a faint photo backdrop and a warm pearl off-white page. The tone is calm and personal, closer to a journal than a review site.

**Live site:** https://exekyute.github.io/table-for-one/

> **Note:** The entries shipped in `index.html` are placeholder (Lorem Ipsum) sample posts that demonstrate the layout. Swap them for real entries to make it your own.

## What it has

- **Minimalist reading layout** built around a single comfortable column and a literary serif (Spectral).
- **Faint fixed backdrop**: a photo sits in a short band pinned to the top and stays in place while you scroll, melting into the pearl base on every edge.
- **Scroll-aware text**: entries rest at full strength in view and softly fade as they leave the top of the screen, returning as they come back.
- **Two-tier archive**: a year row that stays compact over time, with the months of a chosen year revealed below it. Filtering is reflected in the URL.
- **Text search** across all entries, filtering as you type.
- **Per-entry permalinks** with copy-to-clipboard, so a single meal can be linked or bookmarked.
- **Light and dark themes** that follow the system setting, with a manual toggle that is remembered between visits.
- **Place and rating** on each entry, shown as a quiet line under the date.
- **Editorial typography**: a drop cap on each entry and curly quotation marks.
- **Share-ready**: Open Graph and Twitter card tags, a favicon, and a preview image.
- **Print-friendly**: a stylesheet that strips the interface and prints clean text.

## Details

- Works with JavaScript disabled: every entry is in the HTML and remains readable.
- Accessible touches throughout: a skip link, keyboard focus styles, live status messages, and reduced-motion support.
- Newest-first ordering is handled automatically from each entry's date.
